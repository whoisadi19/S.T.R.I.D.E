# Problem Statement: Autonomous Drone-Based Infrastructure Inspection System

## Description
Design and develop an autonomous drone-based system capable of inspecting critical infrastructure using AI-driven analysis, real-time data transmission, and intelligent reporting to enhance safety, efficiency, and accuracy in inspection processes.

The proposed system should utilize drones equipped with cameras and sensors to monitor infrastructure such as bridges, buildings, power lines, pipelines, rail tracks, and industrial facilities.

The system should support the following key functionalities:
• Autonomous flight and navigation along predefined or dynamic routes
• High-resolution image and video capture for inspection
• Real-time data transmission to a monitoring station
• AI-based defect detection and anomaly identification

The system should enable use cases such as:
• Structural inspection of bridges, buildings, and towers
• Monitoring power lines and transmission infrastructure
• Inspection of pipelines and industrial assets
• Hazard detection in unsafe or hard-to-reach environments

Additionally, the system should ensure:
• Safe and stable drone operation in varying environments
• Accurate detection of defects (cracks, corrosion, damage)
• Efficient data collection and processing
• Generation of actionable inspection insights

## Background & Context
Inspection of critical infrastructure is often challenging due to:
• Risks in hazardous or hard-to-reach areas
• High operational costs and time-consuming processes
• Inconsistent inspection quality and human error
• Lack of real-time insights and predictive maintenance

With advancements in drones, AI, and computer vision, there is a growing opportunity to develop intelligent automated inspection systems that improve safety, reduce costs, and provide accurate real-time insights for decision-making.

## Indicative Solution
1. Drone Platform (Hardware)
• UAV with high-resolution cameras and sensors (RGB, thermal, LiDAR optional)
• GPS and IMU for navigation and stabilization
• Onboard processing unit (Jetson Nano / Raspberry Pi / similar)

2. Autonomous Navigation System
• Waypoint-based or AI-driven flight planning
• Obstacle detection and avoidance
• Geo-fencing and safety mechanisms

3. AI-Based Inspection Module
• Computer vision models for defect detection (cracks, rust, damage, leaks)
• Image/video processing and anomaly detection
• Model training using infrastructure datasets

4. Real-Time Communication System
• Live video streaming and telemetry transmission
• Integration with ground control systems
• Cloud/edge-based data processing

5. Dashboard (Web/Admin Panel)
• Real-time monitoring of drone operations
• Visualization of inspection analytics
• Automated report generation

6. Optional Enhancements
• Thermal imaging for heat anomaly detection
• AI-based predictive maintenance insights
• Multi-drone coordination for large-scale inspections

Note: The listed solutions are purely indicative and intended as guidance. Participants are encouraged to implement any subset, the complete set, or propose more innovative and advanced approaches beyond those mentioned. Solutions demonstrating higher levels of completeness, integration, and practical effectiveness will be given due consideration as an important evaluation criterion.

The primary objective of the organizers is to motivate students to attempt real-world, industry-driven problems and gain valuable hands-on experience.
